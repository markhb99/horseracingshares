## What is QTIS?

The Queensland Thoroughbred Incentive Scheme (QTIS) is a bonus prizemoney program administered by Racing Queensland. Its purpose is to reward owners of Queensland-bred and Queensland-foaled thoroughbreds by providing bonus payments above and beyond standard race prizemoney when eligible horses compete in qualifying races in Queensland.

QTIS has grown substantially in recent years as Racing Queensland has invested in growing the local breeding industry. The scheme now covers races at metropolitan venues (Eagle Farm, Doomben) as well as provincial and country tracks across the state.

## How QTIS nomination works

QTIS eligibility requires the horse to be nominated before a set deadline. For foals, the nomination window typically opens at birth and closes before the horse enters training. Nomination fees vary by the tier and the type of nomination:

**QTIS Gold:** The premium nomination for horses by Queensland-based stallions and foaled in Queensland. Eligible for the highest bonus amounts in designated QTIS Gold races.

**QTIS:** The standard tier, available to Queensland-foaled horses regardless of sire location.

Once a horse is nominated by the breeder, the nomination travels with the horse through any sale or syndication. As a buyer of a syndicate share, check the PDS for the horse's QTIS status — a QTIS Gold nomination is a valuable asset that adds significant upside to the horse's earning potential.

## Which races carry QTIS bonuses?

QTIS bonuses apply to a wide range of races in Queensland — metropolitan, provincial, and country. The exact race conditions are published annually by Racing Queensland. QTIS Gold races at Eagle Farm and Doomben are among the most prestigious country and provincial race days in the Queensland calendar, attracting strong fields.

Bonus amounts typically range from:
- QTIS Gold winner (metropolitan): $50,000–$150,000 in additional bonuses
- QTIS standard winner (provincial): $10,000–$40,000 in additional bonuses

Check the current Racing Queensland QTIS schedule for exact figures.

## QTIS and the Magic Millions connection

Queensland is home to the Magic Millions Sales on the Gold Coast every January, and many QTIS-eligible horses are offered through that sale. A horse with both QTIS Gold nomination and Magic Millions Race Series (MMRS) eligibility has access to two separate bonus streams — an attractive combination for owners. ## What to look for in a PDS

When reviewing the PDS for a Queensland horse:
1. Confirm the QTIS tier (Gold or standard)
2. Check whether the annual maintenance fee is included in the disclosed ongoing costs
3. Ask the syndicator which specific QTIS races they plan to target during the horse's campaign

A syndicator with a clear target race plan for a QTIS horse demonstrates that they understand the scheme and are managing the horse's campaign with bonus maximisation in mind.
