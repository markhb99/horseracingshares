## What is BOBS?

The Breeders and Owners Bonus Scheme — commonly known as BOBS — is a bonus prizemoney scheme administered by Racing NSW. It is designed to encourage the breeding and ownership of New South Wales-bred horses by providing substantial bonus payments on top of standard race prizemoney when eligible horses win or place in qualifying races.

BOBS is one of the most generous state bonus schemes in Australia. A country race that carries $20,000 in standard prizemoney can attract an additional $50,000 BOBS bonus for the winner — transforming a modest country race into a $70,000 prize for connections.

## How BOBS nominations work

BOBS operates in two parts: **breeder nominations** and **owner nominations**.

**Breeder nomination** (BOBS Gold): The breeder nominates a foal at birth (or before the nominated close date). This costs approximately $990 (including GST) and makes the horse eligible for BOBS Gold bonuses throughout its career in NSW country and provincial races. The breeder's nomination transfers with the horse — you don't need to re-nominate if you buy a BOBS Gold horse.

**Owner nomination** (BOBS Silver): As the owner, you pay an annual nomination fee (approximately $330 per year including GST) to make the horse eligible for BOBS Silver bonuses. BOBS Silver is a lower-value bonus tier but still provides meaningful additional prizemoney on top of standard race earnings.

Most quality NSW-bred horses offered for syndication will already carry a BOBS Gold breeder nomination. When reviewing a PDS, check whether the horse has Gold, Silver, both, or neither.

## Which races are eligible?

BOBS bonuses apply to country and provincial thoroughbred races in NSW. Metropolitan races (Royal Randwick, Rosehill, Warwick Farm, Canterbury) are not BOBS eligible. The exact race conditions are updated annually by Racing NSW — check their website for the current schedule.

Importantly, BOBS bonuses are paid on a sliding scale based on placing. The winner typically receives the largest bonus, with smaller amounts for second and third place in eligible races.

## How much is the BOBS bonus?

Bonus amounts vary by race conditions and tier. As a guide (amounts change annually):
- BOBS Gold winner at a country race: approximately $50,000–$100,000
- BOBS Gold place at a country race: $8,000–$15,000
- BOBS Silver winner: approximately $5,000–$15,000

These figures are indicative only. The Racing NSW website publishes the current BOBS schedule with exact bonus amounts by race category.

## Why does BOBS matter when choosing a horse?

For a horse racing predominantly in NSW country and provincial races, BOBS eligibility can dramatically change the economics of ownership. A BOBS Gold horse that wins a single country race might earn $70,000 for connections — far outstripping standard race earnings of the same race.

## What to check in the PDS

The PDS should confirm:
1. Whether the horse carries BOBS Gold, Silver, or both
2. Whether the owner nomination fee for BOBS Silver is included in the syndication costs or charged separately
3. That the syndicator will maintain the BOBS nomination annually if required
