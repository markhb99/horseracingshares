## What is VOBIS?

The Victorian Owners and Breeders Incentive Scheme — VOBIS — is a bonus prizemoney program run by Racing Victoria in partnership with Thoroughbred Breeders Victoria. Like BOBS in NSW, VOBIS is designed to incentivise the breeding of Victorian-sired or Victorian-bred horses by offering substantial bonuses on top of standard prizemoney when eligible horses win or place in qualifying races.

VOBIS is arguably the most well-developed state bonus scheme in Australia, and has been running since the 1990s. Awareness of VOBIS among Victorian owners and syndicators is high, and the scheme has been credited with significantly improving the quality and depth of Victorian-bred thoroughbreds over the past three decades.

## The three VOBIS tiers

VOBIS operates across three tiers with different nomination requirements and bonus levels:

**VOBIS Gold:** The premium tier. Requires nomination of the horse by the time it is a yearling. Eligible for races specifically designated as VOBIS Gold races, which carry substantial bonus payments — often $100,000 or more for the winner of a metro VOBIS Gold race. VOBIS Gold races are typically highlight events on the Victorian country and provincial calendar.

**VOBIS:** The standard tier. Available to Victorian-sired or Victorian-bred horses that have been nominated. Bonuses apply to a broad range of country and provincial races in Victoria.

**VOBIS Sires:** Focuses on horses by nominated VOBIS sires. This is a complementary program that encourages use of Victorian-based stallions.

## How to nominate

Breeder nominations for VOBIS must be made while the horse is a weanling or yearling. As a syndicate member, you typically cannot initiate a VOBIS nomination retroactively — the horse either carries a VOBIS nomination already or it doesn't. Check the PDS for the horse's VOBIS status before signing.

Annual owner maintenance fees apply to keep the VOBIS nomination active. These fees are modest (approximately $110–$220 per year depending on the tier) and should be disclosed in the PDS as an ongoing cost.

## Eligible races and bonus amounts

VOBIS bonuses apply to Victorian country and provincial races. The bonus schedule is updated annually by Racing Victoria. Indicative amounts:

- VOBIS Gold race winner (provincial): $100,000–$200,000 in bonus money
- Standard VOBIS winner (country): $10,000–$30,000 in bonus money

These are illustrative figures — check the current Racing Victoria website for precise bonus amounts and eligible race conditions.

## VOBIS and metropolitan racing

Unlike BOBS in NSW, some VOBIS Gold races are held at metropolitan venues (Flemington, Caulfield, Moonee Valley) as highlight race days. The VOBIS Jewels at Mornington, for example, is a major country carnival with VOBIS Gold bonuses attached.

## Checking VOBIS status when buying

Any well-prepared syndicator offering a Victorian-bred horse will include the VOBIS status in the PDS and on their listing. On this platform, VOBIS-nominated horses are tagged in their listing. A horse without VOBIS nomination is not necessarily a poor choice — many successful racehorses are not VOBIS nominated — but the bonus potential is significantly reduced for Victorian country and provincial racing.
