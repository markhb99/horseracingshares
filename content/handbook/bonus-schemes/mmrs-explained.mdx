## What is the Magic Millions Race Series?

The Magic Millions Race Series (MMRS) is a bonus prizemoney program run by Magic Millions, the Gold Coast-based thoroughbred auction house. Unlike BOBS, VOBIS, and QTIS — which are funded and administered by state racing authorities — MMRS is a private scheme funded by Magic Millions as a way of adding value to horses sold through their sales.

The centrepiece of MMRS is the Magic Millions Gold Coast Carnival in January each year, which features a series of high-value races exclusively open to horses that were sold through Magic Millions sales and have been nominated to the Race Series. These races include the Magic Millions 2YO Classic and the Magic Millions 3YO Guineas, each worth $2,000,000 — making them among the richest races for their age group in the world.

## How MMRS eligibility works

A horse becomes eligible for MMRS races if:
1. It was sold through a Magic Millions sale (as a yearling, weanling, or in a select sale), AND
2. It has been nominated to the Magic Millions Race Series

The nomination is typically arranged by the vendor (the stud that sold the horse) at the time of sale. As a syndicate buyer, you should check whether the horse was sold through a Magic Millions sale and whether the Race Series nomination has been maintained.

Annual nomination fees apply to keep the MMRS eligibility active. These fees are usually modest and should be disclosed in the PDS.

## The January Gold Coast Carnival races

The headline events are:

**Magic Millions 2YO Classic (1200m, $2,000,000):** Open to 2-year-olds sold through Magic Millions sales and nominated to the Race Series. The winner receives $1,200,000. Held on a Saturday in January at the Gold Coast racecourse.

**Magic Millions 3YO Guineas (1400m, $2,000,000):** For 3-year-olds with Magic Millions sale provenance. Same prize structure.

**Magic Millions Raceday:** A full day of MMRS races across age groups, making the January carnival a significant national racing event.

Beyond the Gold Coast carnival, MMRS races are held in other states throughout the year — Magic Millions has expanded the series to include qualifying races at provincial venues in Victoria, NSW, and Queensland.

## MMRS outside the Gold Coast Carnival

The Magic Millions Race Series includes races throughout Australia's racing calendar, not just in January. Horses with MMRS eligibility can access bonus races at tracks including Flemington, Randwick, and Eagle Farm during the winter and spring carnivals. This means MMRS eligibility adds value throughout the horse's racing career, not just for the January carnival.

## What MMRS eligibility means for owners

A horse with a Magic Millions sale provenance and a current MMRS nomination has access to some of the richest races for young horses in the world. If your horse shows promise as a 2-year-old, the prospect of targeting the January Gold Coast races adds enormous excitement and potential upside to the ownership experience.

When assessing a syndicate, check:
1. Was the horse sold through a Magic Millions sale? (Check the hip number in the PDS)
2. Is the MMRS nomination current?
3. Does the syndicator plan to target MMRS races, and are they based or licensed in Queensland (or have access to a Queensland trainer)?
