## Why you need to register

Every person who has a beneficial interest in a racehorse in Australia must be registered as an owner with Racing Australia and the relevant state principal racing authority. It is not optional — racing integrity rules require this to ensure that ownership is transparent, and that owner privileges (mounting yard access, raceday benefits) are given only to genuine registered owners.

Registration is straightforward, takes about 20 minutes online, and costs a modest annual fee that varies by state. Your syndicator will typically initiate the process on your behalf and prompt you to complete your part online.

## Step 1 — Confirm your eligibility

You must be 18 or over to register as a racehorse owner in Australia. There are also integrity-related disqualifications — if you have been warned off or excluded from racing in Australia or another jurisdiction, you are not eligible. If in doubt, ask the syndicator before proceeding.

## Step 2 — Go to Racing Australia's online portal

Visit [racingaustralia.horse](https://www.racingaustralia.horse) and navigate to the Owners section. Select "Register as an owner." The portal walks you through the process state by state.

If your horse is trained and racing in Victoria, you register via Racing Victoria. If it is in New South Wales, via Racing NSW. The Racing Australia portal handles cross-state recognition once your home state registration is complete.

## Step 3 — Provide identity documents

You will need to provide proof of identity — typically a scan of your driver's licence or passport — and a proof of address document (a recent utility bill or bank statement). The portal uses an automated identity verification service. The process takes a few minutes online and does not require you to post physical documents.

## Step 4 — Pay the registration fee

Registration fees vary by state and are payable annually. As of 2026:

- **Victoria (Racing Victoria):** approximately $80 per year
- **New South Wales (Racing NSW):** approximately $66 per year
- **Queensland (Racing Queensland):** approximately $60 per year
- **South Australia (Racing SA):** approximately $55 per year
- **Western Australia (Racing and Wagering WA):** approximately $55 per year

Check the relevant authority's website for current fees. Your syndicator may include this cost in the initial setup fee — read the PDS carefully.

## Step 5 — Receive your owner registration number

Once approved (usually within one to two business days), you receive an owner registration number. This number is linked to the horse via the syndicate deed, and it is what entitles you to your raceday owner privileges.

## Step 6 — Obtain your owner's badge

Before raceday, your syndicator or their racing club contact will arrange for you to collect or receive your owner's badge. This badge gets you access to the mounting yard, the owners and trainers enclosure, and the members' area for races in which your horse is declared.

## Tip: keep your registration current

Owner registration is annual. Your syndicator will usually remind you before your registration lapses, but it is your responsibility to renew. If your registration lapses, you lose raceday privileges for that period. Set a reminder in your calendar for 30 days before the renewal date.
